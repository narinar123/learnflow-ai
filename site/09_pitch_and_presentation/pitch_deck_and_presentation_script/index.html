Pitch Deck & Presentation Script

AI Learning & Member Management Platform — LearnFlow AI
Version: 1.0 | Date: July 2026
Format: 15-slide investor/stakeholder presentation

Presentation Overview

Target Audience: Angel investors, seed-stage VCs, potential institutional partners
Duration: 15–20 minutes presentation + 10 minutes Q&A
Narrative Arc: Problem → Solution → Market → Product → Traction → Business Model → Team → Ask

Slide 1: Title Slide

Visual: Full-screen dark navy background with subtle particle animation. Center: LearnFlow AI logo (indigo+purple gradient) + wordmark. Bottom: “Seed Round Presentation — July 2026”

Text:

LearnFlow AI
AI-Powered Learning & Member Management Platform

[Founder Name] | CEO & Co-Founder
learnflow.ai | contact@learnflow.ai
Confidential — Do Not Distribute

Speaker Notes:
“Thank you for your time today. My name is [Name], and I’m going to show you how LearnFlow AI is transforming the way 100 million Indians learn new skills — and why now is the perfect time to build this company.”

Slide 2: The Problem

Visual: Split into 3 panels, each with an icon and a sharp stat

Headline: “Learning has never been more critical — or more broken.”

Panel 1 — Access
Icon: Broken door
“67% of Indians say quality education is too expensive or too far away.”
Source: NSSO 2025

Panel 2 — Completion
Icon: Abandoned path
“Only 15% of online course enrollees ever complete what they start.”
Source: MIT Study, 2024

Panel 3 — Relevance
Icon: Skill gap
“72% of employers say job applicants lack the practical skills they need — even with degrees.”
Source: LinkedIn Workforce Report 2025

Speaker Notes:
“The problem is three-layered: Access — quality education is inaccessible to most. Engagement — even those who access it don’t finish. Relevance — even those who finish aren’t job-ready. Existing platforms solve one of these. We solve all three.”

Slide 3: The Solution

Visual: Platform mockup (phone + tablet + desktop) with animated AI chat bubble overlay

Headline: “LearnFlow AI: Your personal AI tutor + 500+ expert courses + verified certificates.”

Three-column layout:

Column 1 — 🤖 AI That Teaches
“An AI tutor trained on your course content. Explains concepts, generates quizzes, and creates personalized study plans — like having a private tutor 24/7.”

Column 2 — 🎮 Engagement That Works
“XP, levels, badges, streaks, leaderboards, daily quests. The psychology of great games applied to your education. 92% of our users complete their first course.”

Column 3 — 📜 Outcomes That Matter
“Verified certificates, LinkedIn-ready credentials, skill badges. Not just learning — provable learning.”

Speaker Notes:
“LearnFlow AI doesn’t just give you access to content. It surrounds you with a learning experience that’s engaging, personalized, and outcome-focused.”

Slide 4: Market Opportunity

Visual: Concentric circles (TAM/SAM/SOM) with market size labels

Headline: “A ₹85,000 crore opportunity — and it’s just getting started.”

Market Breakdown:

TAM (Total Addressable Market)
“India EdTech Market — ₹85,000 Cr ($10B) by 2030 (RedSeer)”

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
“Upskilling + Online Courses Segment — ₹22,000 Cr ($2.6B)”

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
“Accessible 5-year target — ₹550 Cr ($65M) with 3% market share”

Supporting data:

  • India has 500M+ internet users, 250M+ smartphone learners (IAMAI, 2025)
  • Online upskilling grew 43% YoY post-COVID — structural shift, not a trend
  • 65% of India’s workforce will need reskilling by 2030 (WEF)
  • Global EdTech market: $404B by 2025 (HolonIQ)

Speaker Notes:
“The India EdTech market is not a bubble — it’s a structural shift driven by three irreversible forces: smartphone penetration, increasing demand for upskilling, and an AI revolution that makes personalized tutoring economically viable for the first time.”

Slide 5: Why Now? Why Us?

Visual: Timeline on left, differentiators on right

Headline: “Three tailwinds converge. We’re positioned to ride all three.”

Tailwind 1 — AI Cost Drop
“Inference costs for Gemini/GPT-class models dropped 100x in 2 years. Building an AI tutor for every learner is now economically viable.”

Tailwind 2 — Smartphone Proliferation
“250M+ Indians now have 4G/5G smartphones. They want to learn but can’t afford ₹5 lakh for a classroom program.”

Tailwind 3 — Employer Shift
“63% of top employers now prefer verified skill certificates over degrees for technical roles (NASSCOM 2025). Credentials are being unbundled.”

Our Edge:

  • RAG-powered AI tutor grounded in actual course content (not generic ChatGPT)
  • Cross-platform (Flutter native + React web + Electron desktop) — competitors choose one
  • Gamification engine built from behavioral science, not borrowed from gaming
  • Glassmorphism-first design accessible to teens AND 60-year-olds

Speaker Notes:
“Every piece of the infrastructure needed to build this product at scale just became available in the last 24 months. We didn’t just build a product — we built it at exactly the right moment.”

Slide 6: Product Demo

Visual: Full-screen app screenshots arranged as a walkthrough carousel

Headline: “The product you wish existed when you started learning.”

Screenshot 1: Dashboard — “AI-personalized home, streak tracking, daily quests”
Screenshot 2: AI Chat — “Ask anything about your course, get grounded answers”
Screenshot 3: Video Player — “Professional lesson experience, notes, AI help”
Screenshot 4: Quiz — “AI-generated quizzes with instant explanations”
Screenshot 5: Badge/Level Up — “Gamification that’s actually motivating”
Screenshot 6: Certificate — “Blockchain-verified, LinkedIn-ready certificate”
Screenshot 7: Admin Panel — “Full CRM for institutions and trainers”

Speaker Notes:
“Let me walk you through a day in the life of a LearnFlow AI learner — and you’ll see why our completion rate is 6x the industry average.”

Slide 7: Traction

Visual: Key metrics in large type, trend charts

Headline: “We’re not building this in a vacuum — learners are already here.”

Metrics (if in beta/soft launch):

5,000          Beta users in first 3 months
4.9★           Average App Store rating (127 reviews)
68%            D7 retention (industry avg: 25%)
92%            First course completion rate
₹0             Paid for customer acquisition (100% organic)
12 mins        Average daily time in app
3               Platform partnerships signed (colleges)

Trend chart: Weekly active users over 12 weeks (upward curve)

Speaker Notes:
“These numbers are from our beta period with zero paid marketing. D7 retention of 68% is extraordinary in EdTech — the average is 25%. This tells us we’ve solved the engagement problem.”

Slide 8: Business Model

Visual: Three revenue streams as columns with illustrative flows

Headline: “Multiple monetization layers, all aligned with learner success.”

Revenue Stream 1 — Subscription (Primary)

Free: ₹0/month
Basic: ₹299/month
Pro: ₹599/month
Premium: ₹999/month
Target: 25,000 Pro subscribers by Month 18
Annual: ₹29,940 LTV per Pro subscriber (2-year avg retention)

Revenue Stream 2 — B2B Institution

₹50,000–₹5,00,000/year per institution (50–500 seats)
Target: 100 institutions by Year 2
High NPS, high retention, expansion revenue

Revenue Stream 3 — Trainer Revenue Share

70/30 split (trainer gets 70%)
Average course price: ₹599–₹1,999
Top trainer earns ₹2L+/month — creates supply-side flywheel

Unit Economics:

Pro Subscriber CAC (target): ₹1,200
LTV (2-year): ₹14,376
LTV:CAC Ratio: 12:1 (target by Month 18)
Gross Margin (SaaS revenue): 78%

Speaker Notes:
“Our primary bet is subscription, but the B2B channel has 20x higher ACV and 3x higher retention. We’re designing for both from Day 1.”

Slide 9: Go-to-Market Strategy

Visual: Funnel diagram with channel breakdown

Headline: “Efficient growth through content, community, and word-of-mouth.”

Phase 1 (Months 1–6): Seed

Channel: Content SEO (Python/design tutorial blog posts)
Channel: LinkedIn organic (trainer spotlights, learner stories)
Channel: Product-led growth (free plan → AI upgrade prompts)
Channel: College partnerships (institution pilot programs)
Target: 25,000 free users, 1,000 paid subscribers

Phase 2 (Months 7–12): Grow

Channel: Paid search (Google Ads for course keywords)
Channel: Meta/Instagram (video ads, learner transformation stories)
Channel: Referral program (₹200 credit per successful referral)
Channel: YouTube (course preview clips, "learn X in Y minutes" series)
Target: 100,000 free users, 5,000 paid subscribers

Phase 3 (Months 13–24): Scale

Channel: B2B outbound (enterprise learning & development teams)
Channel: APAC expansion (Southeast Asia)
Channel: OEM partnerships (telcos pre-installing app, banks offering learning perks)
Target: 500,000 free users, 25,000 paid subscribers

Speaker Notes:
“We are not a paid-ads-dependent business. Our CAC today is ₹0 because content, product-led growth, and word-of-mouth are carrying us. Paid ads are an amplifier, not our foundation.”

Slide 10: Financial Projections

Visual: Revenue bar chart (3-year), key metric table below

Headline: “A clear path to ₹10 crore ARR in 24 months.”

Revenue Projections:

Year 1 Year 2 Year 3
Free Users 50,000 250,000 750,000
Paid Subscribers 2,500 20,000 75,000
B2B Institutions 10 100 500
MRR ₹15L ₹1.2Cr ₹4.5Cr
ARR ₹1.8Cr ₹14.4Cr ₹54Cr
Gross Margin 65% 73% 78%

Key Assumptions:

  • B2C CAC: ₹1,200 (paid), ₹300 (organic) — blended ₹600 by Year 2
  • LTV: ₹14,400 (2-year avg for Pro)
  • B2B ACV: ₹1,80,000 (avg 60 seats × ₹3,000/seat/year)
  • Churn: 3.5% monthly (Year 1) → 2% (Year 2+)
  • AI API cost: 8% of revenue (decreasing as models get cheaper)

Disclaimer: Forward-looking projections subject to assumptions and market conditions.

Speaker Notes:
“We’re being conservative. These numbers assume zero viral coefficient and a modest CAC. If we see the referral rates we’re seeing in beta persist at scale, Year 2 could look like Year 3.”

Headline: “No one else is doing all of this, for everyone.”

Competitor Analysis:

Our Moat:

  1. RAG-based AI tutor (trained on actual course content — not generic AI)
  2. India-first pricing with global-quality product
  3. Cross-platform native experience (Flutter + React + Electron)
  4. Behavioral science-backed gamification engine

Speaker Notes:
“Coursera and Udemy own the content library. We compete on experience, AI, and price. Our Indian-first approach and RAG-based AI are our moats — neither can be copied overnight.”

Slide 13: Product Roadmap

Visual: Horizontal timeline with 3 phases

Headline: “Building the definitive AI learning platform — in phases.”

Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–6):
Core platform live. iOS + Android + Web. 100 courses. AI tutor. Payments. Gamification.

Phase 2 — Growth (Months 7–12):
Institution dashboard. Live sessions. Desktop (Electron). Trainer marketplace. LinkedIn integration. 500 courses.

Phase 3 — Scale (Months 13–24):
AI mentor matching. Job board integration. Corporate L&D partnerships. API for third-party LMS. Southeast Asia expansion.

Phase 4 — Vision (Year 3+):
AI-generated courses. Accredited micro-degrees. University partnerships. Real-time skills assessment.

Speaker Notes:
“We ship in two-week sprints. Phase 1 is done. We are raising to fund Phase 2 and Phase 3. Phase 4 is where we become a fundamentally different kind of institution — one that issues credentials that are more valuable than traditional degrees.”

Slide 14: The Ask

Visual: Clean, centered slide with ask amount prominent

Headline: “We’re raising ₹3 crore seed to reach 25,000 paid subscribers.”

Raise Details:

Round: Seed
Amount: ₹3 Crore (approx. $360,000 USD)
Instrument: SAFE / Convertible Note
Valuation Cap: ₹15 Crore (post-money)
Lead investor: [Open / Target: angel + micro-VC]
Timeline: Close in 60 days

Use of Funds:

Marketing & Growth: 25% — Paid acquisition, content marketing
AI Infrastructure: 15% — API costs, RAG tuning, vector DB scaling

Reserve: 5%

Milestones this round buys:

“This is a lean, efficient raise for a specific milestone — getting to 5,000 paying subscribers so we can demonstrate unit economics to a Series A investor. We’ve modeled 3 scenarios and even the conservative scenario shows us hitting profitability on ₹4.5 crore ARR.”
